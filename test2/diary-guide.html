6

Editing & Deleting

From the detail view, tap ✏️ Edit to reopen the full form with all fields pre-filled. Make your changes and save. The entry updates in place — no duplicate created.

Tap 🗑 Delete to permanently remove an entry. You'll be asked to confirm. Deleted entries cannot be recovered.

Warning: Deletion is permanent. If you need a record for compliance, export the entry as PDF before deleting.
7

Season & Ground Management

The diary organises entries by stalking season (August–July). Use the season dropdown in the diary header to switch between seasons.

Grounds (permissions / estates) let you tag entries by where you stalk. To manage grounds:

1. Go to Stats tab → tap Edit on the Season Plan
2. Your saved grounds appear in the "By ground" tab
3. Add new grounds by name — they'll appear in the entry form's ground field

Tip: Grounds persist across seasons. When you start logging entries and tap the ground field, your saved grounds appear as tappable pills.
8

Cull Targets & Season Plan

Set cull targets to track your season plan against actual results. From the Stats tab, tap Edit on the Season Plan card.

Season Targets

Set overall targets per species and sex for the entire season. Use the +/- buttons to adjust.

Ground Targets

Switch to the By Ground tab to set targets per ground. This lets you track allocation across different permissions.

When ground targets are set, filter chips appear above the Season Plan:

Overview
Muntjac Alley
Soignee Wood

Overview shows your season-level targets. Tapping a ground name shows only that ground's targets vs entries tagged to it.

9

Stats & Charts

The Stats tab shows a comprehensive breakdown of your season. All stats respect the species filter chips at the top of the diary.

Stats include:

• Cull map — all entries plotted on an interactive map
• Season Plan — targets vs actual with progress bars
• Monthly cull — bar chart showing activity across the season
• Cull by ground — entries per permission
• Species breakdown — with male/female split
• Calibre usage — which calibres you've used most
• Shot distance — average and range
• Age class — breakdown by age category
• Cull by shooter — if logging for a team
• Carcass destination — where venison went

10

Exporting Data

You can export your records in three ways:

📊Export CSV — all entries as spreadsheet
📄Export PDF — formatted list
📋Season Summary PDF — full report with stats

CSV — opens in Excel, Google Sheets, or any spreadsheet app. Includes all fields.
PDF list — simple formatted list of all entries with key details.
Season Summary — a polished report with header stats, species breakdown, cull plan progress, and a full entry table. Ideal for sending to estates or DMGs.

You can also export a single entry as PDF from its detail view — useful for individual cull records.

Tip: Use the season and ground filter dropdowns in the export modal to export a specific subset of your data.
11

Offline Mode

The diary works without signal. When you're offline:

• New entries are saved to a local queue on your device
• A banner shows how many entries are queued and storage used
• When signal returns, entries sync automatically
• If storage runs low, photos are stripped to save space (you'll be notified on sync)

Note: Photos are large. If you're offline for extended periods, the app may need to strip photos from queued entries to stay within browser storage limits. Entries themselves are always preserved.
12

Account & Privacy

Your diary data is stored securely in Supabase with row-level security — only you can access your entries. No one else, including the developer, can see your data.

From the Stats tab, scroll to the bottom to find:

• Sign out — signs you out on this device
• Delete account — permanently removes your account and all diary data

For full details, read the Privacy Policy.
